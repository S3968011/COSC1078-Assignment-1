Project Idea

My project idea is to create an application that is very simple to use and easily accessible for all. The application will feature a review system where people who attend sports grounds rank their overall experience. Factors such as availability of space, quality of grass/ground, availability of sports equipment (soccer goals, AFL posts), user satisfaction and more are rated on a number system. Once there is data inserted into the application, all users will be able to view this and decide whether this information is useful for them. This may encourage users to frequent parks and sports grounds more regularly as they may find different sports grounds they hadn’t known about and whether they are best suited to their physical activity desires.

Physical activity has been proven to provide great health benefits and improve overall health (Sisson & Katzmarzyk 2008:606). It has been recommended that adolescents participate in 60 minutes of moderate to vigorous level physical activity daily, however, only 33% of adolescents meet these recommendations (Foster et al 2017:68’). With a growing rate of obesity, over the last 30 years especially, it is vital that this major health concern, along with others, are reduced (Valery et al 2012:1).

With the use of this application, finding accessible sports grounds to play sport can improve the physical activity rate in all ages. It is often hard to find soccer fields, football ovals, basketball courts, etc that are not at a decent quality standard or that’s not being used by an organised club. Many sports facilities are owned by a sports club that lock up their soccer goals or take down the AFL posts when members of the club are not using them. This may result in the general public to be discouraged to go outside and participate in various sports that require these types of equipment to play.

The application will rely heavily on user input. A ranking system will be required for users to complete with each submission. It will have multiple areas that are to be ranked from 0-10 stars. The availability of the space, availability of sporting equipment (users are able to add multiple sports equipment and rank them individually), quality of grass/ground and overall satisfaction with experience are all ranked. Comments can be added under each category and an overall comment about the quality of the location can be added, depending on how detailed the user desires it to be.

Users must enable their location while using the app. This informs where the submission has taken place. Once a submission is being made, users will select the park/sports ground they are reviewing within a short radius of their location. If a park/sports ground has not been recognised by the maps function, users are then able to create a new location in order to display this to other users. When not reviewing a location, users are able to browse the map to view different parks/sports grounds. Users are then able to press on a location and view the previously generated reviews that other users have submitted.

A social aspect will be incorporated into the app where users can make posts of what sport they have been playing and where they have been playing it at using their location. Posts will be a different section of the app and act much like how current social medias display their user-generated posts. Users will be able to add their friends by searching their username to view their posts in the social feed section. In addition, specific locations can be followed where users who follow the location will see all posts made with the location being tagged. Users will be able to chat with their friends in a direct messaging system encouraging the participation in social sport activities. People who tagged a certain location are also able to message each other in chat rooms to organise activities such as a soccer match where a group of people are required. This encourages users to participate in more physical activity through being social with the community.

The hardware that is required to successfully create this project is a PC and a smartphone to use the app. The smartphone is to be able to download apps from its application store being the app store or the Google play store. The app will be coded using python to make the features such as the social feed, messaging and review system. Kivy will be used to create the user interface. Google maps API is incorporated so users can access the map function.

If this application is successful and used by the public, it will impact the way in which we communicate with members of our local community. Reviews about sports grounds will be entered into the application frequently by users to allow other locals to know where the best sports ground categorized by sport is located. Overall, this will increase the rate of physical activity, thus, leading to great improvements in health, decreasing risks of various diseases such as obesity.
